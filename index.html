The "AquaPulse" mobile application presents a surveillance system for identifying fish species, behaviours, diseases and water quality impact. The developed application for mobile devices puts consumers' access to the power of smart technology right in their hands. The application, which is accessible with smartphones, is useful for anyone working in the ornamental fish industry. It provides owners with timely notice of fish movement identification, fish species, oviparous fish and dead fish identification. Also, our proposed system provide better health management for fish by identifying fish diseases earlier. Additionally, by setting up recurring reminders for tank cleaning, the app simplifies tank maintenance and keeps fish conditions at their ideal levels. Combining all of these components provides significant benefits for the ornamental fish industry.

Simplified Tank Management

Never miss a tank cleaning again! Aquapulse sends you personalized reminders based on your specific fish species and tank setup.

Intelligent Identification & Classification

Go beyond basic identification. Aquapulse provides insights into fish behavior, including potential breeding activity or signs of stress. This comprehensive understanding empowers you to create an ideal environment for your fish.

Real-Time Monitoring & Alerts

AquaPulse keeps you constantly updated on the well-being of your fish. Receive real-time notifications for critical events like sudden changes in water parameters, detection of deceased fish, or potential disease outbreaks.

LITERATURE SURVEY

Fish behavior has been analyzed using a variety of machine-learning techniques. According to Harkaitz and Martinez's (2023) study, various approaches have been developed for fish behavior identification in aquaculture, including the use of non-intrusive methods such as machine vision, acoustics, and sensors, combined with deep learning techniques for accurate quantification of fish behavior. A biological model and a machine learning algorithm were used to assess Siamese fighting fish foraging performance in a study by Wallace, Moreira, Bessa, et al. (2023). Deep learning methods were employed by Shuxian Wang et al. (2023) to identify the behaviors of Chub mackerel fishing vessels. Abnormal behavior in Larimichthys crocea in recirculating aquaculture systems has been detected using the enhanced YOLOX-S algorithm.

Still, there are gaps in the realm of fish behavior identification. Specifically, there is the requirement for enhanced object detection algorithms that can manage complicated surroundings and high-density scenarios. Another gap is the localization of abnormal behavior, where individual coordinates can be easily ignored, leading to low detection accuracy. Furthermore, limited research has been done on the application of deep neural networks for the real-time identification of abnormal behaviors in fish. 

The current researches focus on various approaches to evaluate aquaculture water quality and clarity utilizing advanced techniques. A deep transfer learning-based classification model was implemented in the study by Hao, Guo, Tao, and Li (2023) for analyzing water-color images for aquaculture water quality assessment. Their approach included gathering a large dataset of 5203 images and categorizing them into five classes based on the color of the water. According to the experimental results in this study, the deep learning model demonstrated its performance in classifying water quality images, achieving an accuracy rate of 99%. This research highlighted the effectiveness of deep learning approaches in automating water quality assessment tasks.The study by Daniel et al. (2021) focused on water clarity assessment of Brazilian inland waters using Sentinel-2 images and machine learning methods. They underlined the significance of Secchi Disk Depth (Zsd) as a crucial water quality indicator. By comparing machine learning algorithms with semi-analytical algorithms, they found that the random forest classifier outperformed traditional methods with less than 22% error. This study demonstrates the effectiveness of machine learning and remote sensing in assessing water clarity. Srinivas et al. (2023) introduced an automated water quality monitoring system using IoT and machine learning approaches.

METHODOLOGY

The mobile application associated with this project serves several key functions. Through the submission of images, videos, and other pertinent data, it enables users to identify abnormal or normal behaviors exhibited by the fish, monitor the quality of water, schedule tank cleaning dates, recognize deceased and oviparous fish, and diagnose fish diseases.

There are four main sections under the methodology.

  1. 1.Fish movement Identification
  2. 2.Fish species, oviparous fish and dead fish identification
  3. 3.Early Disease detection and treatments
  4. 4.Identifying water clarity and calculate the next tank clean date




The overall system diagram of the suggested solution is shown in Fig.1. It is designed to offer an innovative approach for owners of public aquariums, pet stores, aquaculture businesses, hobbyists with home aquariums, and ornamental fish farmers. This system enables users to monitor their fish tanks and analyze fish behaviors and water quality. As shown in Fig. 1, registered users can interact with the mobile application to view real-time data, image captures, and videos. Users can add tanks to their applications and retrieve live data related to their tanks. Our proposed solution uses the YOLOv8 object detection algorithm to identify fish species, oviparous fish species, and dead fish, and it notifies users about oviparous and dead fish. Additionally, the solution identifies diseased fish and recommends treatments for those diseases. This system also considers water quality, using Feedforward Neural Networks (FNNs) and YOLO algorithms for water clarity identification and next tank cleaning date predictions. Data for these purposes is obtained from IoT device readings stored in a Firebase database.

RESEARCH PROBLEM

Combining Water Quality and Behavior Analysis: The ornamental fish industry faces a significant challenge: early detection of diseases that threaten fish health and economic stability. Current methods often focus on individual water quality parameters. This research tackles this limitation by investigating how combined water quality factors influence fish behavior.

Automating Fish Behavior Analysis: Fish behavior can be a valuable indicator of their health. However, manually monitoring behavior is time-consuming and prone to human error. This research addresses this problem by developing a systematic and automated method for analyzing fish behavior. This method could involve cameras, sensors, and machine learning algorithms to capture and interpret subtle changes in movement patterns, potentially leading to earlier disease detection and improved fish welfare.

Identifying Pregnant Fish in Crowded Tanks: Identifying pregnant fish in aquaculture settings can be crucial for breeding programs and maintaining healthy populations. However, traditional methods can be unreliable, especially in crowded tanks. This research explores innovative techniques for identifying pregnant fish. This might involve analyzing body shape changes using web cam images.

Predicting Water Clarity for Optimal Cleaning: Maintaining clean and clear water is essential for fish health. However, determining the optimal time for tank cleaning can be subjective. This research investigates the possibility of using water color analysis to predict water clarity. By analyzing color changes associated with waste buildup, we aim to develop a system that accurately identifies when a tank cleaning is necessary, optimizing cleaning schedules and resource allocation.

Automating Fish Species Identification, Counting Current methods for identifying fish species, counting populations, and detecting dead fish often rely on manual observation, which can be inefficient and prone to error. This research proposes the development and implementation of an automated system. This system could utilize machine learning and computer vision to accurately identify fish species, count individuals, and even detect deceased fish, leading to improved population management and disease outbreak prevention.

Main Objective

The main objective of this study is to develop a comprehensive surveillance system for ornamental fish tank management. This includes the creation of a mobile application that allows ornamental fish farmers and aquarium hobbyists to monitor and maintain optimal water conditions. Specifically, the application will identify water clarity through color changes in the tank water and predict the next tank cleaning date. The purpose of this system is to ensure ornamental fish a healthy environment and improve the efficiency of tank maintenance.


Sub Objective 1

Fish behaviour classification and pregnant fish identification using machine learning algorithms.


Sub Objective 2

Water Clarity Identification & Next tank cleaning date prediction

Sub Objective 3

Identify fish Species count and identified dead fish count


Sub Objective 4

Fish behavior and disease detection accuracy are enhanced by examining water quality parameters, thereby facilitating more reliable predictions for disease outbreaks and fish behavior analysis.

RESEARCH GAP

Even with improvements in the monitoring methods and technology covered above, there is a noticeable gap between research and real-world applications in maintaining ornamental fish tanks. Although research has demonstrated the potential of techniques such as deep learning for evaluating water quality and image processing for analyzing fish behavior, there's little research on how well these methods work in actual fish tank setups in the real world. Furthermore, many of these techniques are challenging for aquarium owners and business experts to implement since they are difficult to utilize or don't scale up well. Therefore, further study is needed to close these gaps and provide a workable, user-friendly ornamental fish tank management system. There are no existing reports on mobile-based monitoring and alerting systems for ornamental fish tanks, this study proposes to develop a mobile application for ornamental fish farmers to monitor fish behaviors and tank conditions.
